Work Queue Scheduling

Periodic Scheduling ScheduleOnInterval(us)

Runs the module at a fixed frequency. Ideal for controllers or estimators needing consistent update rates (e.g., 50Hz control loops).

Delayed One-Shot ScheduleDelayed(us)

Executes once after a specific delay. Useful for initialization sequences, timeouts, or retrying failed operations without blocking.

Event-Driven (Topic Update) Recommended

Using SubscriptionCallbackWorkItem. Wakes up the module immediately when a uORB topic is published. Minimizes latency and CPU usage.

Immediate Execution ScheduleNow()

Places the item at the front of the queue to run as soon as possible. Use sparingly for urgent processing to avoid starving other tasks.

High-Rate Requirements?

For extremely high rates (>1kHz) or strict timing that cannot tolerate queue latency, consider using HRT callbacks directly, but be careful with blocking.

hrt_call_every(&call, 1000, 1000, callback, NULL);
